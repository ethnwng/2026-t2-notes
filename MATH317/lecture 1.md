#### Single Variable Vector Functions
*Defn:* A **vector** $\mathbf{v}$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$  is defined as
$$
\mathbf{v} = a \mathbf{i} + b \mathbf{j} + c \mathbf{k}
$$
where $\mathbf{i,j,k}$ are the unit vectors for each axis, equivalently
$$
\mathbf{v} = < a,b,c>
$$

*Defn:* A **vector valued function** is a function $\mathbf{r}: \mathbb{R}\to \mathbb{R}^3$. That is, a rule that assigns to each real number $t$ in the domain of $\mathbf{r}$ a unique vector 
$$
\mathbf{r}(t) = <f(t), g(t), h(t)>
$$
The real-valued functions $f,g,h$ are called the **component functions** of $\mathbf{r}$.

examples:
- $\mathbf{r}(t) = <\cos(t), \sin t, t>$
- $\mathbf{(t)}=<t\cos t, t,t\sin t>$
